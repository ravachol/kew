@Chromium3Oxide After I got into Linux, I also learned some C programming. I made some terminal apps, which involved ncurses and ansi codes, which is why most of my commits to kew are related to those. I wanted a good terminal music player because mpv and vlc used too much ram, and I tried cmus and mocp, but didn’t like them. Seeking back and forth on cmus and mocp was buggy for me. I eventually found kew by searching enough. Kew actually had good seeking, plus it displayed the album covers and had a cool visualizer. Kew has a small codebase (which I hope stays like this in the future), which is easier to work with and contribute to. Whenever I want to change something in kew, I can just do it, which is what makes kew and open source in general amazing. I also contribute to kew to learn more programming, I guess many open source projects are learning opportunities for their creators. I know maintaining an open source project is hard, so I try to help.
